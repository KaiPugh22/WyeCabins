## The town of books

Known as the town of books, Hay-on-Wye is bursting with independent bookshops and cosy cafes. It's the kind of place where you can lose an entire afternoon browsing shelves, and every street seems to have another surprise tucked around the corner.

## Morning

<img class="blog-photo-left" src="assets/images/richard-booth-bookshop.jpg" alt="Shelves of books inside Richard Booth's Bookshop in Hay-on-Wye" loading="lazy">

Start your day with coffee and breakfast at [The Granary](https://thegranaryhay.co.uk/){{map:"The Granary Hay-on-Wye"}}, right in the heart of town near the Town Clock. Their home-cooked lunches and cakes are worth coming back for later too. After breakfast, wander through Hay's famous bookshops — there are over twenty of them. [Booths](https://boothbooks.co.uk/) is the biggest and most iconic, sprawling through multiple rooms of an old cinema. Addyman's is wonderful for rare and secondhand finds.

## Late morning

Head to [The Old Electric Shop](https://www.oldelectric.co.uk){{map:"The Old Electric Shop Hay-on-Wye"}} for quirky vintage items and gifts — it's part antiques market, part curiosity shop, and entirely charming. If it's a Thursday, the artisan market in the town centre is a must. Local bakers, cheese makers and producers set up stalls and it's a lovely way to soak up the atmosphere.

## Lunch

<img class="blog-photo-right" src="assets/images/tomatitos-tapas-bar.jpg" alt="The timber-beamed bar at Tomatitos in Hay-on-Wye" loading="lazy">

Grab ice cream from [Shepherd's Parlour](https://www.shepherdsparlour.com/){{map:"Shepherds Ice Cream Hay-on-Wye"}}, made with milk from local farms. For something more substantial, [Tomatitos](http://www.haytomatitos.co.uk/){{map:"Tomatitos Hay-on-Wye"}} is a lively local favourite with a great atmosphere, or [The Blue Boar](https://www.tripadvisor.co.uk/Restaurant_Review-g190755-d1880006-Reviews-Blue_Boar_Pub-Hay_on_Wye_Powys_Wales.html){{map:"The Blue Boar Hay-on-Wye"}} is a homely traditional pub with generous portions.

## Afternoon

Walk off lunch along the river. Cross the bridge and follow the path downstream — the views back to the town with the castle above are gorgeous. If you fancy something more active, [Drover Cycles](https://www.drovercycles.co.uk/){{map:"Drover Cycles Hay-on-Wye"}} hire out bikes for the afternoon. For families, the playground by the car park is a good shout while the adults enjoy a coffee.

## Evening

Back at the cabin, light the fire pit, pour a glass of something nice, and watch the stars come out. If you'd rather eat out, [The Old Black Lion](https://www.oldblacklion.co.uk/){{map:"The Old Black Lion Hay-on-Wye"}} does excellent food in a cosy setting, or [Red Indigo](https://redindigo.co.uk/){{map:"Red Indigo Hay-on-Wye"}} is a great option for Indian cuisine.

## Family days out

If you're visiting with children, Cantref Adventure Centre{{map:"Cantref Adventure Centre"}} is about 20–30 minutes away and perfect for both wet and dry days — pony rides, tractor rides, soft play and trampolines. The Small Breeds Centre is a real gem where you can get up close with owls, miniature ponies and pygmy goats. The Brecon Mountain Railway{{map:"Brecon Mountain Railway"}} is a steam train ride alongside the reservoirs, with a museum and play area on site. And the National Showcaves of Wales{{map:"National Showcaves of Wales"}} are a firm favourite with our guests — suitable for all ages and weather, with a dinosaur park for the little ones.
