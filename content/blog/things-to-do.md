## Right on our doorstep

There's no shortage of adventure around Wye Cabins. Whether you fancy a gentle riverside stroll or a proper mountain scramble, the Brecon Beacons has you covered.

## Hay-on-Wye

<img class="blog-photo-right" src="assets/images/5893961312_52762b117d_b.jpg" alt="Hay-on-Wye town with castle above the rooftops" loading="lazy">

A pretty boutique town crammed with vintage shops and booksellers. Don't miss [The Old Electric Shop](https://www.oldelectric.co.uk){{map:"The Old Electric Shop Hay-on-Wye"}} for quirky finds, [Shepherd's Parlour](https://www.shepherdsparlour.com/){{map:"Shepherds Ice Cream Hay-on-Wye"}} for local ice cream and coffee, and the independent bookshops including [Booths](https://boothbooks.co.uk/) and Addyman's. If you're here on a Thursday, the artisan market is well worth a browse for local bakers and producers.

## Talgarth

Just five minutes from our countryside site, Talgarth is a lovely little town with a working watermill — [Talgarth Mill](https://www.talgarthmill.com/){{map:"Talgarth Mill"}} — where you can take a tour and pick up freshly milled flour. There's also a good deli and butchers for stocking up on provisions. For a beautiful walk, head to Pwll Y Wrach (Witches Pool), a magical trail through the woods to a small waterfall.

## Wild swimming & the river

The pebble beach at Glasbury is perfect for a picnic and a dip, and the River Café is right on the bank if you fancy a coffee afterwards. Another favourite is The Warren — a peaceful spot you can reach by walking behind the Three Tuns pub and following the old railway line.

## Hay Bluff & the Black Mountains

Hay Bluff offers a beautiful drive to the foot, then a short but sharp climb to the top with sweeping mountain views. Carry on over the Black Mountains and you'll reach [Llanthony Priory](https://cadw.gov.wales/visit/places-to-visit/llanthony-priory){{map:"Llanthony Priory"}}, a hauntingly beautiful 12th-century ruin with dining available Tuesday to Sunday.

## Walking & cycling

<img class="blog-photo-left" src="assets/images/26285326532_f69d788a4d_b.jpg" alt="Walkers heading up a Brecon Beacons trail" loading="lazy">

The Brecon Beacons are a walker's paradise. Pen-y-Fan, Wales's second-highest peak, is about a 30-minute drive away and the round trip takes 3–4 hours. The Four Waterfalls Walk is around 45 minutes by car and absolutely spectacular. For cycling, [Drover Cycles](https://www.drovercycles.co.uk/){{map:"Drover Cycles Hay-on-Wye"}} in Hay offers road and leisure bikes for hire.

## Adventure activities

There's plenty for thrill seekers too. Wye Valley Canoes and Want to Canoe both offer guided trips on the river. Tregoyd Mountain Riders is just five minutes away for horse riding. The Black Mountains Activity Centre has zip-lining and kayaking, while Interactivities runs gorge adventures and archery sessions. Llangorse Activity Centre has climbing and indoor activities for all weathers.

## Rainy day ideas

[Booths Cinema](https://boothbooks.co.uk/cinema/){{map:"Richard Booth Bookshop Cinema Hay-on-Wye"}} in Hay is a tiny independent with just 40 seats, a bar, and bags of character. Brecon Leisure Centre has swimming, bowling and a gym. The Globe at Hay hosts live entertainment, and Theatr Brycheiniog in Brecon has a regular programme of theatre, comedy and music.
